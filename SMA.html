Overview
Enchantment, romance and magic, San Miguel de Allende has it all. It should come as no surprise that this wonderful city is one of Mexico’s premier destination wedding locations.

This charming, high desert, historical town has much to offer a couple looking for the perfect location to celebrate their destination wedding. In fact, San Miguel, is an excellent choice for taking any special event to the next level.

A beautiful colonial city located at the geographic heart of the Mexico, San Miguel de Allende was declared a world Heritage site, along with the nearby Sanctuary of Atotonilco. The area was occupied in pre-Columbian times before the founding of the colonial town in the mid-16th century. Indeed, most of the city center architecture is a wonderful mix of 18th and 17th century buildings. Originally named San Miguel el Grande, it was re-baptized in 1826 in honor of its native son and hero of independence, Ignacio Allende,

San Miguel has managed to conserve an easy-going, compact, small town feel. If you are staying downtown, you will inevitably find yourself strolling over to the Jardín (the Garden) at the center of the main Zócalo, or town square, to admire the iconic 18th Century Parroquia Church. You will enjoy spending time sitting on a shaded park bench, or at one of the lovely surrounding cafés, observing passers-by, vendors, street performers and the many cultural events that take place on and around the plaza on a regular basis. From there, nearly everything is within walking distance; and if not, taxis are plentiful and inexpensive.

Despite its small size and colonial feel, San Miguel de Allende provides all of the modern comforts and conveniences you would expect at a world-class destination: top- bill entertainment, renowned artists, art galleries, museums, restaurants, quality hotels and shopping.

San Miguel’s’ offerings really do cover the full gamut, from small-town charm to history, high culture and modern conveniences, a perfect combination of for your destination event.
The heart of Mexico
The heart is closely associated with San Miguel de Allende, the reason for this is not just religious, although the “Sacred Heart” is certainly key aspect of the heart symbol in San Miguel. In addition to its religious overtones the heart has also denotes San Miguel’s central geographical location at the heart of Mexico and its historical location at the heart of independence.

As it turns out, coincidentally, as the universal symbol of love and romance, the heart is also the perfect symbol for what is has become one of the most coveted destination wedding locations in the world.

In addition to everything the city has to offer, its unique central location makes it an excellent home base for visitors who want explore the beautiful country of Mexico.

The colonial city and state capital Guanajuato is just 45 mins away, the City of Queretaro, also the capital of a neighboring state, is equally close. Mexico City is just 3 hours away by car. Airports in Queretaro and Leon provide access to more distant points.
Beyond the city limits
There is plenty to do for visitors to San Miguel de Allende interested in outdoor activities. Golf, horseback riding, all-terrain vehicle tours, mountain biking, hot springs, hot air balloon rides are easily accessible in and around the city.

For the more culturally inclined, visits to archaeological sites, historical locations and other artisanal towns are easily arranged. Unique custom tours by local operators are also available.

Travelling foodies will be amazed at the quality of locally produced cheeses, wines and other artisanal food products. In addition to sampling locally produced foods at neighborhood restaurants and quality markets in town, gourmet-food lovers and fans of healthy eating will enjoy a visit to some of the nearby organic farms, producers and vineyards that are open to the public.
Lodging, Restaurants and Shopping
San Miguel de Allende boasts an incredible number of centrally and near center located hotels. There is something for everyone and every budget. Options run from high-end, luxury hotels, spas and short term rental properties, to top of the line boutique hotels and fantastic bed & breakfasts. There are even solutions for the more budget-minded traveler. For those seeking a less urban hotel experience with easy access to town, there are some beautiful options on the outskirts of the city as well.

Restaurants abound in San Miguel de Allende. In addition to excellent home-style and fine dining local and regional cuisine, many international options are also available including continental, Spanish, Italian, French, Thai, and Indian.

The city’s many rooftop bars allow visitors to enjoy San Miguel’s breathtaking views and marvelous sunsets while sipping a delicious margarita or drink of choice, enjoying freshly prepared tapas, or tucking into a full meal.

There is also a variety of shopping options in San Miguel de Allende, whether you hunting for the perfect travel gift, exquisite jewelry, or a lovely decorative touch for your home. Art lovers and history buffs will enjoy the many antiquing options, excellent art galleries and markets. Visitors will also enjoy combing the stores, markets and fairs for fine, handmade local and regional crafts.

San Miguel de Allende has a thriving artist community. For more than 100 years artists have travelled to this high desert city to pursue their calling and hone their craft. Today, art and crafts students continue to flock to its fabled Instituto Allende and Bellas Artes schools to learn from each other and from renowned teachers. Painting has always been a favorite here but sculpture, carving, weaving and jewelry-making are also popular. For those who appreciate art and fine hand-crafted artisanal work, San Miguel offers many galleries, museums and art fairs.

Finally, for those who enjoy live cultural events, it seems that there is always something to celebrate in San Miguel de Allende. There are many local cultural events, indigenous dances and street celebrations year-round. There are also a number of important international annual cultural events that take place in San Miguel de Allende such as the Jazz festival, the International Writers Conference and the International Film festival, to name a few.
