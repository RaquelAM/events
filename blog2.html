Why choose San Miguel de Allende for your destination wedding 
            Long recognized world-wide, and in the United States in particular, as a culture and art center, San Miguel de Allende has been attracting art students and artists seeking inspiration and instruction since the late 1930’s.  It all started in 1938, when Peruvian artist Felipe Cossio del Pomar opened San Miguel’s first art school, the Escuela Universitaria de Bellas Artes in the town center.  It is still housed in the former convent where it was founded. 

            More recently, word of the city’s charms has spread to a wider audience and its popularity as a destination wedding Mecca has really taken off in the last 4 or 5 years. Today, this magnificent World Heritage site attracts, on average, 850 couples annually from all over the world.  
            
            So, chances are, if you have been researching your destination wedding, you have probably heard of San Miguel de Allende. But, you may still be wondering what the fuss is all about.  I hope this post will help answer some of your questions and convince you that 850 couples a year can’t be wrong  !  
            
            All kidding aside, take my word for it, wedding planning is stressful! Especially if you try to go it alone!  So wouldn’t a destination wedding make things even crazier, you ask?   Well, yes, it can.  Unless you plan ahead and you pick the right destination.  
            
            The fact is that, despite the apparent complications, more and more couples are choosing a destination wedding.  I am sure there are many ways to explain this phenomenon, but two main reasons stand out for me. 
            
            1. In this globalized and infinitely connected modern world, people are bored with ordinary options, they are more and more curious about experiencing something new and different. 
            
            2. The professional wedding planner industry has really grown and expanded so that there are more resources available to those couples seeking the adventure of a destination wedding, without the headache.   
          
            San Miguel de Allende covers off both reasons admirably.  First of all, it is a well-preserved and beautiful historical colonial center that transports visitors in time for a magical experience.  Also, its compact size, geographical location and international standing provide easy access to new cultural experiences and adventure. Moreover, as a prized wedding and event destination it has excellent venues, all of the modern conveniences, and, last but not least, experienced consultants and event professionals ready to handle anything.  In other words, when it comes to destination weddings and events, San Miguel de Allende has it all. 
            
            It is incredibly important to consider all of these points when choosing a destination because, typically, a wedding affects more people than just you as the wedding couple. Your plan has to take into account a small to large group of people who may or may not be well-traveled, who are going to vary widely in age, and who may or may not know each other very well, if at all.  Therefore, your destination has to appeal to a wide variety of people, especially if they are paying for the trip themselves or have to take time off from work.  Some of your guests may even want to stay on and make a vacation of it.  So, it is crucial to select a location that will appeal to different age-groups, physical abilities, and tastes. San Miguel de Allende does this very well.  
            
            On the practical side of things, San Miguel has many options for travelers of different tastes and budgets.  Luxury hotels, luxury boutique hotels, Bed & breakfasts, luxury furnished home rentals, as well as options for the budget minded.  And, although the town has preserved a colonial feel, modern conveniences are the rule. Also, your guests need not fear that they will be out of touch from family and friends, as modern means of communication, including the Internet, are widely available.  As for food options, in addition to the wide variety of high quality restaurants, modern hotel concierges and other staff will understand guest needs and will be able to offer knowledgeable advice with regard to special dietary concerns.  Galleries, shopping, live music and excellent bars are all within walking distance in and around the City Center. Taxis are inexpensive, and drivers can be hired for trips beyond the city limits.  
            
            On the more subjective side of things, museums, cultural events, tours and outdoor adventure are readily available. San Miguel de Allende is also the perfect destination for those of your guests who are on the easygoing side, or who enjoy discovering a new city on foot. The main square is where many locals and visitors spend time on a daily basis sitting on shaded bench in the Jardín ( Garden) at its center, or relaxing at one of several surrounding outdoor cafés.   The city itself is a thing of beauty and visitors can spend entire days just strolling through its cobble stone streets, visiting its many squares, parks, markets, and its innumerable churches, fountains and other points of interest. If walking isn’t an option and trolley tour is a fun alternative. 
            
            When it comes to organizing your event, picking your event planner is key to the success of your destination wedding (for tips on planning your destination wedding check out this post:  10 tips for planning the perfect destination wedding  ).  You’ll want to do your homework and pick the right planner for your needs; someone who has exhaustive local knowledge and can help you ensure the comfort and enjoyment of your guests, as well as the success of the event itself.  The good news is that a premier destination like San Miguel de Allende also attract knowledgeable and experienced professionals.  Do your homework, get informed, pick the right team, and then relax in the knowledge that you have made the right choice and can look forward to the beautiful wedding of your dreams in San Miguel de Allende.
