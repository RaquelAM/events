10 tips for planning the perfect destination wedding 
            So the time has come for you to start planning for that special day, the one that will mark the beginning of your lives together as a married couple.  What could be more romantic than a destination wedding?   

            But where do you start? How do you manage it so everyone has a good time and you don't get overwhelmed? Here are some handy tips for planning the perfect destination wedding.

            1. Research, research, research! Research is really your friend when it comes to destination weddings. Even if you have already visited your destination and think you know where you would like to go, our recommendation is that you do your homework and do it early. Go online, check out forums, ask your friends and social media contacts, visit websites that relate to the destination(s) you are considering. 

            2. Factors to consider when choosing your destination: Your and your guests' preferences, tastes, and physical condition; climate, local amenities, transportation, and accessibility; hotels and other lodgings; whether your destination it wedding-friendly and there is a wedding planner available that is likely to meet your needs, etc.

            3. If really you can't decide between two destinations you might want to take an extra initial exploratory trip to each one to see which you like best, but try to come up with one single destination ASAP. 

            4. Contact your wedding planner of choice. Start looking for your wedding planner early. Some questions to ask prospective planners:  are they local? (local is always best)  How well connected tare they or how extensive is their local network?  (for services, venues, suppliers etc.)? How experienced are they? Try to gauge how well they communicate with you and if they are responsive.

            5. Consider your initial timeline. Although we know it is not always possible, in an ideal world you would start your serious planning 6 months before your desired date. The key word is "desired" date, this might change depending on a number of factors, which is why getting started early is always best if you can.
          
            6. Plan to make 3 trips to your destination of choice, before the actual event.  Take you first trip ASAP because many crucial decisions have to be made as early as possible. This trip will involve a meeting with your wedding planner where you will discuss your needs in detail, come to a formal agreement, consider any legal requirements, decide on a date and confirm it with the local official if necessary. Ideally, you will also decide on a venue. During your next two trips, you will continue to work together with the wedding planner and make all of the decisions that you should be present for.

            7. Choosing the right date for your wedding will make all the difference and a good wedding planner will help you make this important decision. You must find a date that works for you, your guests, and takes into account local conditions. Make sure you consult with your wedding planner on this issue, understanding local conditions is essential.

            8.  Menu planning and tasting. During one of your subsequent trips, you will make your final decisions regarding the menu and you will have a menu tasting. Before you reach that point, you will already have discussed your food and serving preferences with your wedding planner. Since this is a destination wedding, it is likely that your guests will want to try the local cuisine, but don't take that for granted. There are also special dietary needs to consider. Your wedding planner will help you with this process so that, on the day, everyone will come away happy and satisfied.

            9. Make the most out of your trips and face to face meetings with your wedding planner. Although technology makes it easy to connect and simplifies matters considerably, some things must or should be done in person such as date selection, venue choice, and of course menu tasting, as well as some elements of decoration and design.

            10. Trust and communicate with your wedding planner. Talk about what you really want and how you see your event unfolding.  If your wedding planner is any good, he or she will quickly grasp the result you are looking for, will give you sound advice, and will know how to make it happen. Remember, that your planner's objective is to give you your dream destination wedding. A true professional will go for nothing less than perfection. 

            11. BONUS TIP!  REMEMBER TO HAVE FUN! Planning a destination wedding or event can seem overwhelming, but that is why you have wedding planners! A destination wedding is supposed to be fun and different. Remember to enjoy yourself during the planning process as well!
